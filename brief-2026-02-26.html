Something runs through today's brief that systems theorists would recognise: the whole differs meaningfully from the sum of its parts. A school practicing mindfulness school-wide is not just a school with mindfulness added. A consciousness that Damasio and Seth can't fully explain is not just a brain with extra steps. When Marketers Go Too Far with AI: How to Balance Automation With Authenticity
A web agency writing honestly about the failure mode: AI-generated content that is technically competent and perceptibly hollow. The piece names the specific damage — not just underperformance but active reputation harm from content that signals inattention — and proposes a practical framework for where AI generates and where humans shape, edit, and inject lived experience. Curriculum Planning and Implementation for Mindfulness Education and Diversified Humanism
A 2025 study in Nature's open-access Scientific Reports tracks the measurable outcomes of a mindfulness-integrated curriculum: not just wellbeing improvements but gains in focus, creativity, self-confidence, and teamwork. The finding worth sitting with is that mindfulness education, when designed carefully rather than grafted on, changes the fundamental quality of student engagement — not just how students feel, but how they participate.

Education
2024
System-wide School Mindfulness: Addressing Elementary Students' Social-Emotional Learning and Wellbeing
What happens when mindfulness isn't a class or an add-on but a property of the whole school? A Frontiers in Education study traces the difference between mindfulness as programme and mindfulness as culture — and finds that the systemic version produces qualitatively different results. Teachers change their practice, children carry the skills between contexts, and the improvements in resilience compound across the school day rather than appearing only in dedicated sessions.

Emergence as a Science
A December 2025 paper in Frontiers in Complex Systems argues that emergence is not just a concept but a discipline — one that needs its own frameworks and methods rather than borrowing piecemeal from physics, biology, and philosophy. The author identifies nine distinct emergence "prototypes," each with different properties and implications. Worth reading alongside AI development: whether LLM capabilities "emerge" or are gradually acquired is one of the live debates in machine learning right now.

What's So Hard About Understanding Consciousness?
Nautilus brought Damasio and Anil Seth into dialogue on the hard problem — and the conversation is more honest about uncertainty than most consciousness science writing. Both are serious, both have theories, neither claims to have resolved the question. What makes the piece useful for a practitioner is the implicit message: the science and the practice are asking different versions of the same question, and neither has arrived.

Aeon
November 2025
Why You Need Your Whole Body — From Head to Toes — to Think
A November 2025 Aeon essay attacking brain-centrism at its root. The author, a cognitive scientist, argues that cognition doesn't happen in the brain — it happens in the body, and the brain is one organ among many that participates in the process. The evidence builds from cellular biology upward: even single cells without nervous systems solve the fundamental problem of staying alive. What we call "thinking" may be a property of the whole organism, not the organ.
