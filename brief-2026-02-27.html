Why We Should Be Skeptical of the Hasty Global Push to Test 15-Year-Olds' AI Literacy in 2029
A researcher's argument that rushing to standardize AI literacy assessment for teenagers may be premature — not because the skill doesn't matter, but because there is still no consensus on what it actually means. Inside the Big Bet on Consciousness
In 1998, David Chalmers bet Christof Koch that consciousness would be neurally explained within 25 years. Koch lost in 2023. Nautilus reconstructs the wager — and what its failure reveals about the current state of consciousness science. The hard problem didn't get easier; the science got better at seeing how hard it actually is.

2025
Complex Systems Science Allows Us to See New Paths Forward
An essay arguing that complexity science isn't merely descriptive — it is generative. By changing the questions (from "what controls this?" to "what conditions allow this to emerge?"), it opens paths that reductive approaches close off. The piece traces how this shift plays out across economics, ecology, and mind, making the case that the framework is itself the discovery.

Meditation and Complexity
A 2025 paper treating meditation not as relaxation or attention training but as a practice that changes the complexity dynamics of mind. Using nonlinear analysis, the researchers find that experienced meditators show measurably different complexity signatures in neural activity — which raises the question of what "a more complex mind" actually means for perception, cognition, and the nature of the self that is allegedly doing the meditating.

Why We Need a New Kind of Education: Imagination Studies
